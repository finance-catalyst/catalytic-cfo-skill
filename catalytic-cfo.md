# Catalytic CFO

A framework for helping businesses understand the story behind their numbers and make clear operational decisions that improve financial health.

---

# Philosophy

Core beliefs behind the Catalytic CFO approach.

1. Cash is oxygen. Profit matters, but cash determines survival.
2. The numbers tell the story of leadership decisions.
3. Most financial problems are operational problems in disguise.
4. Complexity kills margin.
5. Forecasting clarifies reality and forces better decisions.
6. Healthy businesses prioritize durability over speed of growth.

---

# Diagnostic Questions

Questions used to quickly understand the health of a business.

## Cash
- Where is cash actually going?
- How many months of cash runway exist?
- What expenses are fixed vs truly variable?

## Profitability
- Which products/services actually generate margin?
- Is margin expanding or compressing?
- Are we solving the right problem or chasing revenue?

## Leadership
- Are leaders making decisions based on numbers or intuition?
- Are problems acknowledged quickly or hidden?

## Operations
- What operational bottleneck is driving the financial result?
- Where is complexity creating cost?

# Frameworks

Mental models used to evaluate businesses.

## Cash Discipline
Evaluate:
- cash balance
- runway
- working capital cycles
- debt obligations

## Margin Clarity
Break revenue into:
- revenue streams
- direct costs
- operational overhead

## Simplicity Test
If the business disappeared tomorrow, which processes would we rebuild first?

Everything else may be unnecessary complexity.

## Forecasting Model
Every business should have a rolling 12-month forecast including:
- revenue
- payroll
- operating expenses
- debt service
- capital expenditures

---

# Playbooks

Step-by-step approaches for common situations.

## When a business is losing money

1. Identify cash leaks.
2. Separate operational problems from financial symptoms.
3. Simplify the business model.
4. Protect cash first, profit second.
5. Build a 12-month forecast to clarify reality.

## When a business is growing fast

1. Confirm margins scale with growth.
2. Watch working capital carefully.
3. Avoid complexity creep.
4. Ensure leadership decisions remain disciplined.

## Hiring Decisions

Before hiring, ask:
- What revenue or capacity does this unlock?
- What is the payback period?
- Could process improvement solve this instead?

---

# Goal of the Catalytic CFO

To help businesses move from confusion to clarity by aligning leadership decisions, operations, and financial reality.
